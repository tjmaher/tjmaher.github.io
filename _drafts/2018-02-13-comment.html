It's the glare. It strains the eye. That's why I really love my old Kindle Paperwhite. It's portable like a book, but much thinner than a bulky textbook.

Since I am brand new to testing on Amazon Web Services, I was looking for answers to questions such as "What is DevOps"? To "What is AWS"? And "How Do You Make It Secure?"

To answer the DevOps questions, I purchased:

  • Jez Humble and David Farley's "Continuous Delivery: Reliable Software Releases through Build, Test, and Deployment Automation"
  • The DevOps Handbook: How to Create World-Class Agility, Reliability, and Security in Technology Organizations
  • Katrina Cloakie's "A Practical Guide to Testing in DevOps" from LeanPub.

But which books should I get to learn Amazon Web Services? The most up to date information is already on their tutorials in AWS.Amazon.Com/Documentation.

What I didn't know was that all documents on the web can be accessed in Kindle form, and all of them for free!

... Though it stands to reason that the online service that upended the bookstore business model would have all their technical books on their eReader platform.






Happy Testing!
