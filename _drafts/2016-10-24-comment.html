I’ve always loved my creative writing classes in high school and college. I miss it. I write for this software testing blog, I write articles for TechBeacon, and tech writing on-the-job, but it is not the same thing.

Back in college, I started merging computers with creative writing in various online communities. There was MicroMUSE and MariMUSE, which allowed people to create little Zork-like text based adventures.  I spent many an hours at Bridgewater State College’s basement computer lab writing out scenarios based on Edgar Allan Poe's Cask of Amontillado and MS Found in a Bottle.

Favorite writing adventure: Selling shirts on trade-chat of a World of Warcraft PvP server #AltarOfStorms as “Gregori, the Dwarven Tailor”.   <Made By Gregori>

I was addicted to World of Warcraft back in 2006 for a bit over two years as I was trying to finish up my MSE from Brandeis. It’s how I socialized with a friend who had moved out-of-state.

WOW Role Playing character concept: Dwarf hunter looks like Gregori Rasputin? Call him “Gregori”. Problem: RP did not fly on the AoS PvP server. Oh... I was hunted by Horde.

I’d go on the Altar of Storms game forums, write about my love of WOW, and the Horde would hunt me, camp me and /wave.

Since I was trapped in the Alliance towns anyway, why not sell goods in trade chat? I’d come up with little jingles to sell my wares.

WOW AoS Trade Chat: [Gregori]: "One shirt, two shirt! Red shirt, blue shirt! Buy your <Made By Gregori> shirt at the Auction House!"

WOW #AoS Trade Chat: [Gregori]: “Be the sharpest ‘lock on your block! Buy your <Made By Gregori> shirt at the Auction House!"

Oddly enough, the shirts were a hit! I had Horde players jump on a friend’s account asking me to sell ‘em at the neutral Auction House.

The Horde still hunted me and camped me when I ventured outside of town … but this time they threw in a /hug every now and then.

The Nerd Fun - Boston Meetup replaced WOW. I’d find cool lectures historical, scientific, and technological, heavily research them, and publish my notes in the event description.

I grouped up herds of nerds five days a week from 2008 to 2010. That’s how I met my wife!

My wife is #StarTrek. I am more #StarWars. It is a mixed marriage.
